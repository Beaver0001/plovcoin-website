Chapter One

From every kitchen
to the blockchain

Plov didn't belong to one place. It appeared, independently, wherever fire met grain met meat met spice. A universal recipe written not by one chef, but by humanity itself.

~ 500 BC

The First Pot

Rice meets fire meets meat. The recipe writes itself across continents — no patent, no owner, just the obvious answer to "what to cook tonight".

10th century

The Spread

Trade routes carry the recipe. Each culture adds its signature — saffron here, raisins there, lamb, beef, chicken, vegetables. Same idea, infinite variations.

2003

UNESCO Heritage

Recognized as humanity's intangible cultural heritage. Not because one place owns it — because everyone does. A communal recipe scaled across borders.

2026

On-Chain

A recipe that survived 1,500 years of distributed consensus moves to Solana. Same philosophy, new technology. Open source, community-owned, immutable.
